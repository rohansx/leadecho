# Chrome Web Store — Screenshot Guide

The Chrome Web Store requires **5 screenshots** at 1280×800 or 640×400 pixels (PNG or JPEG).
Screenshots must show the extension in action on real or realistic pages.

---

## Screenshot 1 — Side Panel: Leads Tab

**Page:** Any Reddit post page that matches a pain-point keyword.
**Setup:** Open the LeadEcho side panel (click the extension icon → "Open side panel"). Switch to the **Leads** tab. Ensure at least 3-5 lead cards are visible with:
- Platform badge (e.g. "Reddit")
- Intent label (e.g. "buy signal")
- Truncated post content
- "Open ↗" link

**Crop:** The full browser window showing Reddit post on the left and the side panel open on the right.
**Caption idea:** "Leads from 7 platforms, scored and ranked in your side panel"

---

## Screenshot 2 — Dashboard: Smart Inbox

**Page:** LeadEcho dashboard at `/inbox`
**Setup:** Ensure at least 6-8 mention cards are visible in the inbox, spread across "Leads Ready" and "Worth Watching" tiers. Show the filter bar at the top (platform + intent dropdowns). Ideally show one card expanded with AI intent label, relevance score badge, and the "Draft Reply" button.

**Crop:** Full dashboard view including sidebar navigation and inbox content area.
**Caption idea:** "AI-scored inbox — highest-intent leads always at the top"

## Screenshot 3 — Side Panel: Reply Queue + Post Now

**Page:** A Reddit or Twitter page (any).
**Setup:** In the LeadEcho dashboard, approve 2-3 draft replies. Open the side panel Queue tab. Show reply cards with:
- Platform badge + post title
- Reply preview text
- "Post Now" button (blue/green)

**Crop:** Browser with side panel open, Queue tab active, showing 2-3 reply cards ready to post.
**Caption idea:** "Approve replies in the dashboard, post with one click from the extension"

## Screenshot 4 — Dashboard: Analytics + UTM Tracking

**Page:** LeadEcho dashboard at `/analytics`
**Setup:** Scroll down to the UTM Tracking Links section. Show:
- At least 2-3 UTM short links in the table (`/r/abc123`)
- Click counts populated
- The "New Link" button in the header
- The KPI cards at the top of the page (Mentions 30d, Converted, etc.)

**Crop:** The analytics page showing both the KPI row at top and the UTM links table at bottom.
**Caption idea:** "Close the attribution loop — track which social replies convert"

## Screenshot 5 — Setup: Settings / Onboarding

**Page:** LeadEcho dashboard onboarding wizard (step 2 or 3) **or** the extension Settings tab.
**Option A (Onboarding):** Show the 4-step wizard at step 2 (pain points input), with the progress bar showing step 2 of 4 active. Fill in realistic pain points (e.g. "can't find qualified leads on LinkedIn, spending too much time on cold outreach").
**Option B (Extension Settings):** Show the extension Settings tab with a backend URL and masked API key filled in, and the green connection status dot in the header.

**Crop:** Full browser window for Option A; or browser with side panel for Option B.
**Caption idea (A):** "Define pain points in plain English — no keyword lists needed"
**Caption idea (B):** "Connect to your self-hosted backend in 30 seconds"

---

## General Tips

- Use a dark browser theme to match LeadEcho's dark UI
- Clear the browser address bar or blur it before screenshotting
- Make sure no personal email addresses or real usernames are visible (use demo/test data)
- Chrome's built-in screenshot tool: DevTools → ⋮ → "Capture screenshot" (full page at any resolution)
- Resize Chrome window to exactly 1280×800 before capturing for best results
